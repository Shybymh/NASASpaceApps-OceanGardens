What is the Ocean?

The Earth's surface is predominantly covered by the ocean, accounting for 70 percent of it. This vast body of water comprises
roughly 1.35 billion cubic kilometers (equivalent to 324 million cubic miles), representing approximately 97 percent of the total
water found on our planet. It is the ocean that sustains all life on Earth and gives our planet its distinctive blue appearance when
observed from outer space. Among the celestial bodies in our solar system, Earth stands alone as the only one confirmed to possess
liquid water.

The ocean is the largest reservoir of carbon in the world. Phytoplankton take part in photosynthesis and, thus, help with carbon sequestration. In turn, they release oxygen.

Fun Facts

1. Did you know? What we currently know about the ocean is nothing but a small information compared to the vastness of what's unknown. In fact, it's said that only 10% of the world's oceans have been mapped, and because of this, the amount of sea creatures discovered is also extremely small! Think of all the creatures living in the parts of the ocean not yet discovered, maybe you could be the one to discover them all!


2. According to studies, if all the ice in the world melted, the sea levels would rise by about 70 meters! It would be as high as the Statue of Liberty!


3. As surprising as it sounds, thanks to oceans most of our planet is dark! In fact, despite what we see everyday, the sea levels reach up to 12,100 feet deep, and light naturally doesn't get that far underwater. Since our world is made up mainly of oceans, this means we are more likely to be in a very dark place than under a bright light!
